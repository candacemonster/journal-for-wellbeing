Big life audits can feel overwhelming. Monthly soft check-ins are smaller and kinder. They give you a chance, once a month, to ask "How am I, really?" and to adjust gently rather than waiting for a crisis.

You can use these prompts at the end or beginning of each month. Answer as many or as few as you like.

---

### January: Beginnings and Energy

- "How does my body feel about the idea of a new year right now?"
- "What is one thing I actually have energy for this month, and one thing I do not?"
- "If I chose one word to guide January, it might be…"

These questions focus on realistic energy rather than pressure for total reinvention.

---

### February: Relationships and Care

- "Where do I feel most supported in my life right now?"
- "Where do I feel lonely or unseen?"
- "What is one small way I can show care to myself or someone else this month?"

February can be a month to notice connection and disconnection without judgment.

---

### March: Emerging and Adjustment

- "What is beginning to shift or thaw in me as the seasons change?"
- "Is there a habit or expectation from winter that I want to release?"
- "What would 'a little more light' look like in my daily routine?"

These prompts mirror the slow, uneven emergence of spring.

---

### April: Curiosity and Experimentation

- "What am I curious about exploring emotionally this month?"
- "If I treated my life like a gentle experiment, what would I try?"
- "Where am I taking myself too seriously, and what would softening 10 percent look like?"

April can be about small experiments, not big reinventions.

---

### May: Capacity and Boundaries

- "What is currently taking more capacity than I have available?"
- "Where might a boundary, even a small one, help protect my energy?"
- "What is one thing I can say no to or limit this month?"

These prompts help you see where your time and energy are leaking.

---

### June: Pleasure and Presence

- "What has actually felt good in my body or life lately, even if it was small?"
- "How did I make space, or not, for rest and enjoyment this spring?"
- "What is one tiny pleasure I want to weave into this month?"

Focusing on small, embodied moments can support regulation and joy.

---

### July: Pace and Expectations

- "Is my life moving faster, slower, or about the same as I wish it were?"
- "Where am I demanding a pace from myself that my body cannot keep?"
- "What would it look like to move 10 percent slower this month?"

These questions invite you to renegotiate speed and pressure.

---

### August: Reflection and Adjustment

- "What has this year taught me so far about what I need?"
- "What is one area where I want to adjust my expectations for the rest of the year?"
- "Where have I surprised myself in how I coped or grew?"

August can be a soft mid-year reflection.

---

### September: Transitions and Routines

- "What transitions am I navigating right now, big or small?"
- "What routines are supporting me, and which ones are draining me?"
- "What is one tiny routine I would like to begin or revive this month?"

Transitions often stir up old feelings. These prompts help you notice and respond.

---

### October: Inner Landscape

- "If my inner world this month were a landscape, what would it look like?"
- "What emotions seem to be visiting most often lately?"
- "What parts of myself feel like they are asking for more attention?"

Describing emotions as images or places can deepen awareness.

---

### November: Gratitude and Honesty

- "What am I genuinely grateful for, even if it is small or imperfect?"
- "What am I not grateful for, and can I allow that truth without shame?"
- "What is one way I can thank myself for how I have carried me this year?"

This is not about forced gratitude, but about honest noticing.

---

### December: Closure and Carrying Forward

- "What do I want to gently put down from this year?"
- "What do I want to carry forward with me into the next season?"
- "If I wrote a compassionate sentence about how I did this year, it would say…"

---

Monthly soft check-ins like these help you adjust gradually, instead of swinging between ignoring your needs and trying to overhaul everything at once.

You can adapt these prompts for different years and seasons. The goal is not to answer perfectly, it is to show up once a month and listen.

## Privacy Policy

**Last updated: March 3, 2026**

Journal for Wellbeing values your privacy. This policy explains what information is collected when you visit this site and how it is used.

### 1. Cookies

This site uses cookies — small text files stored in your browser — to serve advertising and analyse traffic. A cookie consent notice is shown on your first visit. You may accept or decline non-essential cookies at any time.

**Types of cookies used on this site:**

| Cookie type | Purpose | Set by |
|---|---|---|
| Essential | Basic site function (none currently set by this site itself) | — |
| Analytics | Understand traffic patterns (pages visited, session length) | Google Analytics (GA4) |
| Advertising | Serve and personalise ads based on your interests and browsing history | Google AdSense |

### 2. Google AdSense & Advertising

This site is a participant in the Google AdSense programme. Google and its partners use cookies (including the `DSID`, `IDE`, and `_gcl_*` cookies) to serve ads based on your prior visits to this and other websites.

- Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit here and/or prior browsing activity.
- You may opt out of personalised advertising by visiting [Google Ads Settings](https://www.google.com/settings/ads).
- Alternatively, opt out via [www.aboutads.info](https://www.aboutads.info/) or [www.youronlinechoices.eu](https://www.youronlinechoices.eu/) (EEA users).
- Read more about how Google uses information from sites that use its services: [How Google uses data](https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites).

### 3. Google Analytics

This site uses Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand general usage patterns such as page popularity and traffic sources. Analytics data is aggregated and does not personally identify you. IP addresses are anonymised by default in GA4.

### 4. Your Rights (EEA / UK / California)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or California, you have the right to:

- Access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you.
- Object to or restrict how your data is processed.
- Withdraw consent for non-essential cookies at any time (use the "Decline" option in the cookie banner or clear your browser cookies).

This site does not directly collect, store, or sell personal data. For data held by Google (Analytics or AdSense), please refer to [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

### 5. Embedded Content & Outbound Links

Some pages may include embedded content (such as images hosted on third-party services) or links to external websites. These services may set their own cookies or collect data independently of Journal for Wellbeing.

### 6. Managing Cookies

You can manage or disable cookies at any time in your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect how this site and its advertising function. For guidance, visit [www.allaboutcookies.org](https://www.allaboutcookies.org/).
